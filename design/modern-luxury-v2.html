IA note on trust

Why the search almost always fails the couple.

Every wedding photographer's portfolio is the same portfolio: thirty perfect frames, from the best day of their year, lit gold and cut to music. It is real, and it tells you almost nothing. It cannot show you the median wedding — the one shot in a crowded Jayanagar hall at eleven at night, when the plan has slipped by two hours and the family is tired. That is the wedding you are actually hiring for.

So couples fall back on the only signals they have. A hashtag on Instagram. A number a planner passes along — often for a cut you never see. A cousin who was happy enough. And the gap between the reel and the delivery is exactly where it goes wrong: the lead shooter you met at the pitch, replaced on the day by someone you didn't; the eight-month wait for an album; the drive of files that arrives thinner than the promise.

The problem was never too few photographers. It was no way to know which ones keep their word.

Wedding Gully begins from the opposite end. We keep the category deliberately narrow — for now, only photographers and film studios, only in Bengaluru — because trust does not scale by the thousand. Before a studio is ever shown to you, we have sat across from the people behind it, watched complete and unedited galleries from real recent weddings, and spoken to the couples who lived through their delivery.

What you meet here, then, is not a marketplace of options. It is a shortlist someone has already stood behind — so the only judgement left to you is the one that should be yours alone: whose way of seeing feels like yours. Begin your search below.
